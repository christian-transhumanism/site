Critical Rationalism is the epistemology associated with philosopher [[Karl Popper]], and recently with physicist [[David Deutsch]]. It explains how knowledge grows, and why science works as well as it does. The basic premise is that knowledge grows through conjecture and critique, rather than through induction. For this reason, scientific theories are designed to be tested and *falsified*, rather than demonstrated or proven. The basic process:

## How Knowledge Grows

**Conjecture**
We propose explanations of how the world works.

**Critique**
We test them hard, and let the weak ones fall.

**Correction**
Better explanations survive; understanding grows.

**Openness**
Nothing is ever proven beyond all question.
